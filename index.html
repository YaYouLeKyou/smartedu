About the Project

Project Acronym: Smart edu AI

The project "SMART TRANSFORMATION IN EDUCATION WITH ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE" (Smart edu AI) is a Small-scale partnership in school education (KA210-SCH) under the Erasmus+ Programme. It has a duration of 24 months, starting on January 1, 2025, and ending on December 31, 2026, with a total lump sum budget of €60,000

Main Objectives and Priorities: The project aims to integrate Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies into educational processes to support individual learning and development. Its core objectives are:
• Using Student-Centered AI Applications in the Learning Process: Offering personalized learning experiences tailored to individual styles and paces through adaptive learning technologies.
• AI Training for Teachers: Enhancing teachers' digital skills to enable them to create more personalized and effective learning environments.
• Individual Learning Analysis and Feedback Mechanism: Utilizing AI to analyze student learning data, identify strengths and weaknesses, and adapt teaching strategies accordingly.
• Ensuring Equality of Opportunity in Education: Employing AI technologies to create an equitable learning environment for disadvantaged students, helping overcome barriers like language differences and special needs

Smart Edu AI

The project titled "SMART TRANSFORMATION IN EDUCATION WITH ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE" (Smart edu AI) will run for 24 months from January 1, 2025, to December 31, 2026. Its main goal is to support personalized learning and development by integrating artificial intelligence (AI) technologies into education. The project focuses on developing student-centered AI applications, training teachers in AI tools, and ensuring equal educational opportunities. It also emphasizes individual learning analysis, data analytics, and sustainable environment skills.

Aligned with Erasmus+ priorities, the project addresses digital transformation, environmental sustainability, climate action, and teacher support. Key themes include AI and data use, ecological skills, inclusion, equality, and non-discrimination.Target groups include students across primary, secondary, and high school levels, especially disadvantaged learners, students with special needs, language barriers, and gifted students. Teachers, educational institutions, administrators, and policymakers are also involved.

Key activities involve AI-assisted training and workshops for teachers and students, carbon footprint reduction and sustainability workshops, AI applications in digital design and music practice, AI-supported teaching for disadvantaged students, augmented reality and simulation science experiments, and feedback sessions. Four main activities are led by partners in France, Spain, Turkey, and Poland.

Expected outcomes include enhanced student learning experiences and academic performance, improved digital literacy for teachers, a data repository, innovative teaching methods, and increased environmental awareness. AI integration will personalize education, improve efficiency, and reduce teachers' workload.

The coordinating organization is Eurolab for Teachers (Poland), with partner organizations France Culture Connection (France), Düzce Milli Egemenlik Bilim ve Sanat Merkezi (Turkey), and Centro Público Integrado O Cruce (Spain).

AI Training for Educators (France)


The "Smart Edu AI: A 5-Day Training Program for Youth Workers" is designed to empower educators and students through Artificial Intelligence. This international program, taking place from July 21st to 25th, 2025, in Strasbourg, France, involves partner organizations from Poland, Türkiye, France, and Spain, fostering global collaboration.

The program aims to provide practical training through hands-on workshops and group activities, focusing on pedagogical innovation and promoting responsible and sustainable AI use.

Day 1: Building Foundations

Opening speeches, icebreakers, and partner presentations set the stage. Expert talks define AI and explore its applications in education, introducing key technologies like Machine Learning, NLP, and Computer Vision. Discussions focus on why AI matters in education—enhancing accessibility, efficiency, and engagement.

Day 2: Empowering Educators and Learners

Focuses on integrating AI into lessons using pedagogical models such as Gagné's 9 Events and the 5E Model. Training on AI-based educational tools like ChatGPT, Quizlet, and Google Classroom. Group activities explore student-centered AI applications.

Day 3: Managing Education with AI

Covers project management tools like Trello, Slack, and Asana for educational projects, along with AI-based performance tracking. Ethical considerations such as data privacy, bias, and transparency are key discussions.

Day 4: Learning by Doing

Hands-on AI workshops, AR/VR exploration, and gamification activities. Includes an AI for Data Analytics Workshop and discussions on how AI can support continuous teacher growth.

Day 5: Transforming Education with AI

Innovation contest, project presentations, and feedback sessions. Discussion on AI-based learning analytics, sustainable technology, and the future role of educators in an AI-powered world.
